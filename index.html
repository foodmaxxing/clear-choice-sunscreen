Why Mineral Sunscreen Is the Right Choice in 2025

There's a reason dermatologists and clean beauty advocates keep pointing people back toward mineral-based formulas. The FDA has confirmed zinc oxide and titanium dioxide as the only two sunscreen ingredients currently recognized as both safe and effective. Meanwhile, a growing body of research has raised questions about common chemical UV filters — including oxybenzone and homosalate — related to skin absorption and potential hormonal activity at higher concentrations.

Beyond the regulatory picture, zinc oxide has a meaningful technical advantage: it's the single broadest-spectrum UV blocker available. Unlike titanium dioxide, which is strong against UVB but trails off at longer UVA wavelengths, zinc oxide covers UVB, UVA II, and UVA I with near-uniform protection. UVA rays are the ones responsible for photoaging — the wrinkles, the sun spots, the long-term cumulative damage that doesn't announce itself as a burn but shows up years later.

If you're going to wear one product every morning that earns its place on your skin, a mineral formula anchored by zinc oxide is the scientifically defensible choice.
